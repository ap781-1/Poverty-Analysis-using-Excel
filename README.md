Multi-Dimensional Poverty Analysis
Introduction
Economic development is often measured by improvements in income, living standards, and economic productivity. One commonly used indicator is Gross National Income (GNI) per capita adjusted for Purchasing Power Parity (PPP), which allows comparisons of income levels across countries by accounting for differences in price levels.
This project analyzes the evolution of GNI per capita (PPP) across countries between 1990 and 2020 using World Bank data. The goal is to explore patterns of global development, compare income growth across countries, and identify which regions or economies experienced the most significant improvements in living standards over time.
By examining long-term trends in income, the project aims to highlight disparities in development, identify fast-growing economies, and better understand the dynamics of global economic progress.
